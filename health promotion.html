TYPE ONE DIABETES SUPPORT FOR KIDS AND TEENS (ToDS4KaTs)

Community based ToD information and management program, offering both physical and telemedicine platforms for targeted information on ToD as well as evidence based, management of ToD of kids and teens (KaTs) living in the informal settlements of Kenya.

ToDS4KaTs Centre

A physical centre where children and adolescents can access Level 3B and referral services for further management of ToD.

OptimizedToDS4KaTs

A virtual platform developed in collaboration with kids and teenagers (KaTs) and University students. It provides:-
- Interactive, age and culturally appropriate ToD information and education
- Direct link to community-based first responders – teams of dedicated, trained and equipped community health promoters (CHP).
- Tele-medical support to CHP for appropriate management of KaTs living with ToD. Professional support is offered by a team of medical volunteers, via a free, private virtual network

ToDS4KaTs Institute

A physical and virtual recruitment and capacity building platform for:-
- Recruitment of Community Health Promoters (CHP) to meet the increasing needs of the KaTs llving with ToD in a given community
- Training of recruited CHP using the Government of Kenya approved CHP basic modules handbook and other approved ToD training resources.

ToDS4KaTs Centre

A physical centre where children and adolescents can access Level 3B and referral services for further management of ToD.

Care-givers and Family Empowerment (CAFÉ) forum

Offers the following opportunities:-

Table banking for ToD

Aims to reduce financial burdens associated with taking care KaTs living with ToD.
Table banking is a group funding strategy where CAFÉ members meet periodically, place their savings, loan repayments and other contributions on the table before them and then proceed to borrow immediately or later, either as long or short-term loans according to individual interest and available funds.

Urban farming

Aims to increase access to healthy and appropriate nutrition by KaTs living with ToD.
CAFÉ members grow foods to feed themselves, the community, and sometimes selling it for some money to address other needs for KaTs living with ToD.
Urban farming occurs when people living in a city or heavily populated towns repurpose their small green spaces or potential environmental pollutants (e.g. plastic bottles, sacks, motor vehicle tyres) to grow food (fruits and vegetables) and/or raise smaller animals (e.g. goats, rabbits, chickens, turkeys).
Due to the informal nature of the dwellings of our target population, not every urban farm is located at the owners’ housing e.g. backyards, rooftops or indoors; some are located on leased/common community spaces.

ToD information and Education

Using physical and virtual platforms to provide ToD information and education for families and caregivers of KaTs living with ToD

Networking and Linkages

Between families/caregivers and CHP in the communities for comprehensive management of KaTs living with ToD.
