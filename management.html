COSMIC

A Comprehensive Sickle cell disease (SCD) management and information centre (COSMIC) is a project of Yes to Kids (Y2K) Foundation, a Kenyan Non-Governmental Organization (NGO).


Vision

Children with SCD living to their full potential across the lifespan

Mission Statement

Promoting evidence-based service delivery for prevention of adverse health outcomes among children living with SCD

Core values

a) Patient centred

Children living with SCD together with their parents, caregivers and families are treated with dignity, and involved in all decisions about their health.
 

b) Holistic

All health services are geared towards supporting the overall well-being of children living with SCD — such as physical, emotional, social and spiritual health — and not just treating a health issue by itself.
 

c) Evidence - based

Quality care offered to children with SCD is based on the most up-to-date research and knowledge
 

d) Rights-based

Focusing on underlying determinants of health – the economic, political, and social systems that determine health status and have far greater impact on health of children than mere provision of medicine.
 

e) Equitable

Every child living SCD is supported to attain their full potential of health, and no one is disadvantaged due to race, ethnicity, age, disability, socioeconomic status, or geographical background.

Strategies

Advocacy

1. Raising awareness and encouraging action on SCD
 
2. Strengthening of workforce of hematologists and other health care professionals to optimally treat patients with SCD throughout all of its stages: from early diagnosis, through childhood, and into adulthood
 
3. Free access to hydroxyurea and other biomedical products for optimal care of children living with SCD

Service delivery

1. Voluntary and Provider initiated SCD screening - Antenatal, Newborn and Premarital screening.
 
2. Evidence based SCD management
 
3. Sickle cell support groups - providing psychosocial support to children living with SCD and their parents/caregivers

Capacity building for SCD

1. CHP (Community health promoters) - sickle cell disease training module for in-service CHP
 
2. CHOW (Community health on wheels) for VOC (Vaso-Occlusive crisis) - training for community-based boda boda riders to serve as first responders during VOC
 
3. Continuous professional development (CPD) for hematologists and other health-care providers who may treat children living with SCD

Research

Conducting relevant research and supporting access to clinical trials for children living with SCD, to promote evidence-based service delivery

Innovation 

24 hour Vaso Occlusive Crisis Drop-in Centres (VOC-DICE) are aimed at mitigating suffering associated with vaso-occlusive crisis
