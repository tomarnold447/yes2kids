COSMIC HEMODRIVE

HEMODRIVE

Yes to Kids (Y2K) Foundation through its innovative HemoDrive is strategy to complement Kenya transfusion and transplant authority (KTTA) efforts, by recruiting university students in Kenya, as new and retained, blood donors.
Existing evidence suggests that University students appear to be good candidates for long-term regular blood donation. However, addressing misconceptions, enhancing knowledge, creating convenient opportunities, and fostering a positive social environment is essential for promoting blood donation among students in Kenya.

Purpose

To mitigate the blood deficit in Kenya by inspiring new blood donors among the university students, to step forward and encouraging existing donors to continue their life-saving contributions.

Factors that impact blood donation among university students

Knowledge and Awareness: Lack of information about blood donation can deter students from participating. Cosmic HemoDrive organizes educational campaigns and awareness programs to play a significant role in dispelling myths, fears (pain, disease transmission, HIV status) and increasing knowledge on blood donation. When students are well-informed about the process, benefits, and safety, they are more likely to donate.

Opportunity and Accessibility: Students who have never been approached for blood donation may not actively seek out opportunities. Cosmic HemoDrive efforts in the university campuses avail convenient and accessible opportunities to encourage blood donation and donor retention.

Social and Peer Influence: Cosmic HemoDrive clubs domiciled within the universities positively influence friends and peers to participate in blood donation. They promote blood donation as a socially responsible act, thus motivating students to donate blood when called to do so.

Course of Study and Awareness: influencing blood donation vary based on the field of study. Cosmic HemoDrive reaches all the students with blood donation information during university years that potentially impacts their blood donation and donor retention behaviours.

Implementation Strategies

Cosmic HemoDrive

A national, varsities-wide blood donation drive, help on the World blood donor day, followed by ceremonies recognizing exemplary blood donors disaggregated in different categories, namely: -
i. Individual HemoDrivers: platinum, gold, silver, and bronzes awards
ii. Institutional HemoDrivers: platinum, gold, silver, bronzes awards
iii. Corporate HemoDrivers: platinum, gold, silver, bronzes awards

Cosmic HemoDrive Registry (CHDR)

A blood group registry of university students developed through the provision of free, on-going blood grouping services in the universities.
AI-generated short, motivational messages on blood donation are conveyed periodically to registered students, to recruit new donors and prevent repeat donors from lapsing and eventually becoming inactive.
CHDR app creates blood group cohorts for targeted blood donation appeals through linked blood donation canters in Kenya.

Cosmic HemoDrive Rush

Are high impact, Gen-Z friendly educational and motivational campaigns held periodically to encourage voluntary blood donation and promote donor retention within the universities.

Cosmic HemoDrive (CHD) clubs

Vehicles for mobilization of blood donors and donors’ retention within Universities in Kenya.
